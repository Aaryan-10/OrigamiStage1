Interesting Facts About Camel

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  2. Camel's ears are furry.
  3. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  1. Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  2. Chameleons are tree-huggers.
  3. Unlike many lizards,chameleons can't regrow their tails.

Interesting Facts About Cicada

  1. Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
  2. Humans eat them, too.
  3. More than 3,000 species exist.

Interesting Facts About Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  2. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  3. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.
  4. Their navigational skills also make pigeons great long-distance messengers.

Interesting Facts About Panda

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  3. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  4. A 45-kilo adult (and pandas can reach 150 kg in captivity) spends as long as 14 hours eating. And it can eat 12 to 38 kilos of bamboo a day.
  5. A panda Can Poop 28 Kilos per day!

Interesting Facts About Teddy-Bear

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  3. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss who is known for her minuscule work.
  4. The world's largest stitched teddy bear is proudly American! The 55-foot-4 creation was made in 2008 and is named C.T.Dreams, which stands for Connect the Dreams. You can go visit it in Wichita, Kansas!
